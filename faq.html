FAQ

01.

MEDICINE: IS THERE A DOCTOR OR HOSPITAL NEARBY?

A medical worker will be present during the whole camp and will assist the children if necessary. We also have a special vehicle for quick transportation to the nearest hospital in case of urgent need.

02.

WHAT TO PUT IN THE BAG?

Your child should pack comfortable clothing suitable for outdoor activities, including a raincoat, sturdy shoes or hiking boots, a hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, a refillable water bottle, a flashlight, and any necessary personal items and medications. A detailed packing list will be provided after registration.

03.

ARE THE CHILDREN SAFE AT THE CAMPSITE?

Yes, children's safety is our top priority. Our campsite is secure, and we maintain a strict supervision ratio of 1 adult to 8 children. All our staff undergo background checks and are trained in first aid and emergency procedures. The campsite is also regularly inspected for safety hazards.

04.

IS IT COLD IN THE TENT?

Our tents are designed to provide proper insulation. During summer months, the tents are comfortable, but we recommend bringing a warm sleeping bag as temperatures can drop at night. In case of unusually cold weather, we have heating solutions available.

05.

WHAT ARE THE LIVING CONDITIONS FOR CHILDREN?

Children stay in modern, waterproof tents with raised platforms to keep them dry. We provide comfortable sleeping mats, and children should bring their own sleeping bags. Clean toilet and shower facilities are available at the campsite. All meals are prepared fresh daily by our certified cook.

06.

HOW ARE WATER ACTIVITIES ORGANIZED?

Water activities take place under strict supervision by certified instructors. All children must wear life jackets during water activities, regardless of swimming ability. We assess each child's swimming level at the beginning of camp and organize activities appropriate to their skill level.

07.

WHAT KIND OF FOOD A KID IS ALLOWED TO TAKE WITH HIM?

We provide all necessary meals and snacks. Children can bring a small amount of non-perishable snacks, but please avoid foods that require refrigeration, contain nuts, or are high in sugar. We ask that you inform us of any dietary restrictions or allergies during registration.

08.

WHAT ABOUT HYGIENE IN THE CAMPSITE?

We maintain high hygiene standards with daily cleaning of facilities. Shower and toilet facilities are available and cleaned multiple times a day. Hand washing stations are set up throughout the camp, and we ensure children follow proper hygiene practices, especially before meals.

09.

WHERE ARE CHILDREN'S BELONGINGS KEPT?

Each child has designated storage space in their tent for personal belongings. Valuable items can be stored securely with the camp staff. We recommend labeling all items with your child's name to prevent loss. Lost and found items are collected daily and returned to children.

10.

ARE CHILDREN SAFE FROM INSECTS AND PESTS?

Our tents have mesh screens to keep insects out. We treat the campsite area to minimize pests and teach children about avoiding contact with wildlife. We recommend bringing insect repellent, and our medical staff is prepared to handle any insect bites or allergic reactions.
