OUR STRUCTURE

The Ekiya Rover Crew is structured to promote effective leadership, clear communication, and distributed responsibility. Our organizational structure allows us to efficiently manage our activities and make the most impact in our community.

THE COUNCIL

The Council is the highest decision-making body within the Ekiya Rover Crew. It comprises elected leaders who provide strategic direction, oversee the implementation of plans, and ensure that the Crew operates in alignment with its mission and vision. Council members serve as the primary representatives of the Crew in external engagements and are responsible for maintaining relationships with the Starehe community and other partners.

THE COH (COURT OF HONOR)

The Court of Honor (COH) is responsible for the day-to-day operations of the Crew. It comprises patrol leaders and committee heads who work together to implement the Council's decisions, coordinate activities, and address any issues that arise within the Crew. The COH also plays a crucial role in maintaining discipline and ensuring that members adhere to the Crew's code of conduct.

PATROLS

Patrols are the foundational units of the Ekiya Rover Crew. Each patrol consists of a small group of members led by a patrol leader. Patrols facilitate closer bonds among members, encourage teamwork, and provide a platform for developing leadership skills. They participate in activities together and often take on specific responsibilities or projects within the larger Crew.

PUBLIC RELATIONS COMMITTEE

The Public Relations Committee is responsible for managing the Crew's image and communication with external stakeholders. They handle social media accounts, create and distribute promotional materials, and coordinate media coverage for the Crew's activities. The committee also maintains relationships with partners, sponsors, and the wider community.

DESIGN TEAM

The Design Team creates visual content for the Crew's communications, events, and initiatives. This includes designing posters, social media graphics, merchandise, and other promotional materials. The team ensures that all visual content aligns with the Crew's brand identity and effectively communicates its message.

INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS

The International Relations Committee establishes and maintains connections with scouting organizations and other relevant groups outside Kenya. They coordinate international exchanges, identify opportunities for members to participate in global events, and facilitate cross-cultural learning experiences to broaden the horizons of Crew members.

CAREER GROUPS

Career Groups bring together members pursuing similar career paths to provide mutual support, mentorship, and professional development opportunities. These groups organize career-specific workshops, connect members with industry professionals, and facilitate internship and job placements to enhance members' career prospects.

DANCE TEAM

The Dance Team develops and performs choreographed routines for Crew events and external performances. They represent the Crew in dance competitions, cultural shows, and other public events. The team provides a creative outlet for members interested in dance and contributes to the Crew's cultural identity.

FOOTBALL TEAM

The Ekiya Football Team represents the Crew in football tournaments and friendly matches with other organizations. The team promotes physical fitness, teamwork, and sportsmanship among members. Regular training sessions and matches provide an opportunity for members to build camaraderie while engaging in healthy competition.
